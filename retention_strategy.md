# Retention Strategy & RFM Segments

## 1. Customer Segments Identified
Based on our RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) calculations and support ticket history, we have segmented our customer base into the following cohorts:

**Segment 1: Champions**
* **Behavior:** Purchased recently (<30 days), purchase frequently, and have high lifetime value.
* **Retention Action:** Do NOT give discounts. Offer early access to new product launches or a VIP loyalty tier upgrade.
* **Expected Value:** Protects high margins while increasing brand advocacy.

**Segment 2: High-Value Unhappy (At Risk)**
* **Behavior:** Spent high amounts historically, but haven't purchased in over 90 days AND have a history of >2 support tickets.
* **Retention Action:** Personalized outreach from a senior customer success agent. Offer a high-value replacement or a "we apologize" credit.

## 2. Campaign Budget Prioritization
If given a limited campaign budget, I would prioritize the **High-Value Unhappy (At Risk)** segment first. It is significantly more expensive to acquire a net-new customer than to retain an existing one. Because these customers have already proven their willingness to spend large amounts, resolving their specific friction points offers the highest immediate ROI for the business.
